Brock the Brick

This story is completely irrelevant, but it is a story I enjoy telling when people inquire about the brick with googley eyes that sits on my shelf. Brock originated from the brick path that led up to UMD's 251 North Dining Hall. One of the bricks on that path became loose one day and was subsequently stolen by a student. In the following days, the bricks around the hole left by the missing brick also became loose, and all those bricks were stolen by students too. And of course, I had to get one as a momento of my time here at UMD, and that's how Brock the Brick came to be. Following the event of the stolen bricks, UMD had to patch the wide hole left behind. But before the cement glue solidified under the newly placed bricks, those new bricks got stolen too! UMD tried once more to cement down new bricks, but again, the bricks were stolen. Finally, UMD just patched the hole with black asphalt. So, if you ever go to 251 North Dining Hall and see an asphalt patch on the floor, you now know how that happened.
